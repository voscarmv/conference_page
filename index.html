NET.ART
WORLD EXHIBIT
2020

A gathering of the most influential net artists from all around the world.
Experience the newest in net.art at GLITCH ~/

2020.10.17(SAT) ~ 18(SUN)

@Mestna galerija in Ljubljana

Main program
Exhibition
Experience and explore the latest works and art installations by our guest artists.
Meet the artists
A press-conference-style round table where the artists will take questions from GLITCH ~/ attendants.
Lunch
Vegan, outdoor picnic-style lunch. Meet and greet other net art fans while you enjoy a delicious meal.
Workshop
Learn some of the techniques developed by our guest artists in this interactive hands-on net.art workshop.
Afterparty
Join us in the evening for music and drinks in a friendly, social setting. Some of the artists will attend as well.
Guest artists
JODI art collective
Authors of the award-winning net.art site wwwwwwwww.jodi.org
Jodi is a collective of two internet artists, Joan Heemskerk (Netherlands) and Dirk Paesmans (Belgium). They were some of the first artists to create Web art.
Alexei Shulgin
Known for his ongoing so called "386DX" performances.
Alexei Shulginis a Russian born contemporary artist, musician, and online curator. In 1994, he founded Moscow-WWW-Art-Lab WWW Art Lab, collaborating with many artists from London and Slovenia. Since 2004, Shulgin has been a co-owner of Electroboutique.
Genco Gulan
Genco uses text, codes and even his own DNA in his art.
Genco Gulan is a contemporary conceptual artist and theorist, who lives and works in Istanbul. His experimental works include net-art, web art, A.I.gen (generated) images, Robot Games, SCIgen papers and online videos. His work often carries political, social and/or cultural messages.
Lorna Mills
Her work explores how "the notion of public decency is anachronistic"
Lorna Mills is a Canadian net.art and new media artist who is known for her digital animations, videos, and GIFs. Mills has done work in other mediums such as installations. She currently lives and works in Toronto, Canada.
Grégory Chatonsky
Founder of incident.net, an Internet art collective exploring the notions of the accidental, the glitch and unpredictability.
Grégory Chatonsky is a French and Canadian Artist who works with interactive installations, networked devices, photographs and sculptures. He explores the relationship between technologies and affectivity creating new forms of fiction.
Vuk Ćosić
Ćosić uses ASCII characters to form images or videos.
Vuk Ćosić is a Slovenian contemporary artist. Active in politics, literature and art, Ćosić has exhibited, published, and been active since 1994. He is well known for his challenging, ground-breaking work as a pioneer in the field of net.art.
